Figure: Daily COVID-19 values (dotted) vs 7-day rolling average (solid). Data cleaned by capping extreme spikes.

Insights

- Spikes in the data have been mitigated by removing data points greater than 3 standard deviation
- Using the rolling average we can see easily trends in the data
- Correlation does not mean causation
- dotted line for the daily numbers because they are much more erratic, giving the focus onto the average
- Trends in all countries is that cases rise during the hotter months, and fall during the colder months
- Rises and falls are based on contraction rates in warmer times vs colder
- Could also be the rates at which people are reporting the sickness in the months vs not
- As for vaccinations we see a large rise starting in 2021, up to 2023, then a rise again in 2024
- Likely due to the population getting booster shots or some other factor
